About Lingenia

This is an online generator for creating a complete Conglang from scratch. You can choose the number of vowels and consonants that you want your language to have (or let the program generate them from a distribution of the most common numbers). The program will then generate the phoneme components of the language, based on the probability of each phoneme occuring in a real world language and put those together into words. Grammar rules are generated last, so it's possible to 'glue' together words into sentences.

Of course, at each stage, you can choose to specify the generation rules, phonemes or grammar rules yourself if you would prefer.

I created this project partly because I wanted to try programming an automatic Conlang generator (I am too lazy and uncreative to choose lists of phonemes and grammar and come up with words myself) and partly as a project to learn some web programming. Even though the results are a long way off a properly built language by a conlanger or linguist, I hope it's still of use if you're just looking for some naming language words for a writing or gaming project.

The processes and rules I have used to create a natural language are based on the excellent 'How to make a Language' series from Biblaridion on Youtube (foundhere). I chose to use this method because it breaks everything down into easy steps and also outlines some nice rules of thumb, which translate well to code.

The files containing probabilities of phonemes (both vowels and consontants) is modified from the brilliant Phoible database: Moran, Steven & McCloy, Daniel (eds.) 2019., PHOIBLE 2.0., Jena: Max Planck Institute for the Science of Human History. (Available online athttp://phoible.org, Accessed on 2020-07 and 2020-08.)

And finally, this is an open-source project, so feel free to get involved with pull requests or your own fork. I'm a bit of a newbie at this (both Conlanging and open-source programming), so any contributions are welcome!
